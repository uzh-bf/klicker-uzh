Fall Release 2020

Dear users of KlickerUZH,

Highlights

This section highlights the most important features that have been released since our spring release. Detailed information on the current state of development is always available on Github.

Participant Authentication

Thanks to a sponsorship by the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Informatics at UZH, we could implement capabilities for participant authentication. Users of KlickerUZH can now restrict access to their sessions to a set of predefined participants. Participants can be handed out simple username/password combinations, or can be required to log in through AAI.

Currently, responses cannot be associated with the corresponding participant (i.e., responses are always secret and only stored in aggregate). However, we are thinking about optional ways of making the association transparent to lecturers, while still ensuring proper privacy of participant data.

For more detailed information, please refer to our documentation for the feature.

Outlook

Regarding longer term development, we are currently evaluating new use cases for improving digital interactions and feedback/reflection on sessions. If you have any wishes or specific requirements regarding any of the areas below, please let us know.
  • Gamification: Driving engagement with gamified interactions.
  • Data Analytics: Integrated capabilities for data analysis.
  • Feedback-Channel: Extended real-time feedback capabilities.
  • Authentication: Support SWITCH edu-ID and other providers.
  • Easier Self-Hosting: Improvements to documentation and architecture.
  • Architectural Improvements: Improvements to performance and readiness for cloud deployment.

The KlickerUZH is developed by the Teaching Center at the Department of Banking and Finance, University of Zurich. Contact us via klicker.support@uzh.ch.
