LaTeX Formulas

In collaboration with the Distance and Independent Studies Center at TU Kaiserslautern, we have developed a new version of the question content editor in the KlickerUZH. We thank the DISC for their support in bringing these improvements to life.

The new question content editor supports the following additional use cases:

  • Lecturers can embed inline or centered formulas in LaTeX notation (e.g., $$\frac{1}{2}$$) in question content and SC/MC answer options. Formulas will display appropriately on the participant and evaluation screens.
  • Lecturers can preview their questions while editing. 
  • Lecturers can format single words as code elements and can display lines formatted as blockquotes (e.g., with indentation).
  • The underline formatting option has been removed. Existing question contents have been adapted to use italic where underline was used.

Furthermore, this update will allow us to incorporate additional formatting options (e.g., hightlighted code snippets) more easily in the future. If you have specific requirements, please leave them on our public roadmap. For more details on the functionality, see our new documentation or try it yourself!

Real-time Feedback and Use Cases

Throughout the fall semester, we have continued our work on the audience interaction functionalities. Students are now (again) able to provide direct real-time feedback on the speed and difficulty of the lecture contents, and lecturers can view the aggregated feedbacks in their session cockpit and on the dedicated lecturer cockpit. For all the functionalities supported by the new Audience Interaction, refer to our documentation.

Our project website has been extended with use cases for the two audience interaction concepts that we have evaluated with lecturers and students:

  • Live Q&A
  • Real-Time Feedback

Each use case includes the goals and possible scenarios, as well as results based on an initial survey and evaluations gathered from lecturers and students. We will continue to evaluate the functionalities and update the results periodically.

Gamification

We have started working on the second phase of our P-8 project, which is focused on the incorporation of gamification in teaching scenarios. We are collaborating with the P-8 project on game-based learning (https://www.gbl.uzh.ch) to finally provide you with a knowledge base with essentials on gamification and game-based learning (e.g., know-how, technical and didactic resources and frameworks).
The KlickerUZH will also directly support several gamification concepts (the simplest example being gamified polls) and we will evaluate these concepts in lectures to provide you with applicable know-how and best practice recommendations.

To incorporate your experiences and requirements regarding gamification and game-based learning in our projects, we have created a short survey on both topics. It would help us tremendously if you could spend 10 minutes to fill-in the survey on https://ref.bf-app.ch/gamification.

For more information on the P-8 project and what it might bring for you, we have created a Roadmap that will be extended throughout the project. Also, we publish regular project updates on our blog and in our community.
