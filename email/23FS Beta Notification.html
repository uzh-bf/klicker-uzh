KlickerUZH Inside Spring 2023

Release v3.0 Beta (UZH)

Dear KlickerUZH users, 

We hope you have had a good start into the new year! Since time never stands still and learning environments are constantly changing, we are happy to announce a complete redesign of the KlickerUZH with many new functionalities being released publicly in August 2023. The current version of KlickerUZH will be available until the end of September. A migration of all data will be possible between both versions. 

The highlights of the KlickerUZH v3.0 are: 

  • Gamified Live Quizzes (e.g., with leaderboards and timed challenges) 

  • Microlearning (new smartphone app) and Practice Quizzes (in OLAT) 

  • Integration with Microsoft PowerPoint (embed evaluations and session control) 

  • Groups and Collaborative Group Activities 

  • Gamified Courses (e.g., with course-level leaderboard and achievements) 

If you want to become one of the first users of the new KlickerUZH and are interested in testing our new platform before the public release, we would like to invite you to a private beta test (UZH-only) during the upcoming spring term 2023.  

The remainder of our newsletter presents more details on the new concepts covered in the new update, as well as information on the beta testing and release schedule, our migration plan, and what you can expect from our project in the future. 

Thanks for your interest and we hope to see you soon as part of our beta test. 

Best regards, 

Your KlickerUZH team 

Summary of KlickerUZH v3.0

Since its inception, the KlickerUZH has been focused on synchronous in-class interaction (I.e., polling). In the past years, we have added many features that further support this specific use case (e.g., Live Q&A and Real-time Feedback). However, a large part of students learning journey has now shifted away from in-class settings and been replaced with blended learning or even fully e-learning courses.  

Therefore, the goal of the new KlickerUZH v3.0 is to support asynchronous interaction and learning use cases with our new Practice Quizzes, Microlearning and Collaborative Group Activities, as well as to gamify selected elements (e.g.,  Live Quizzes) or all KlickerUZH activities in an entire course as a «Challenge». The KlickerUZH v3.0 is also available as a smartphone app and will be published in popular app stores. 

We have received promising feedback from the extensive pilot test in our  own assessment course «Banking and Finance I» with ~700 students  This pilot will accompany the same cohort with additional functionalities (e.g., achievements and a storyline) in the continuing course «Banking and Finance II» in the spring term 2023. 

In addition to our internal pilot, we invite all University of Zurich users  to participate in our private beta testing. For more details on joining the beta test, please read on. 

Our community post on https://community.klicker.uzh.ch/t/klickeruzh-v3-0-concept-and-request-for-feedback/79 and the posts linked therein provide a more in-depth overview on these concepts, in case you are interested.

Beta Testing and Release Schedule

The beta testing of KlickerUZH v3.0 is open to all users at the University of Zurich. We will begin onboarding new users at the beginning of February. With personal onboarding sessions we can introduce the added functionalities tailored to your use case and  gather any specific requirements for the platform. 

To participate in the beta test, please refer to the detailed instructions in https://community.klicker.uzh.ch/t/sign-up-for-the-klickeruzh-v3-0-uzh-beta/101.  

After the conclusion of our private beta test, we will finalize the implementation of the tested functionalities and start working towards the final release, which is currently scheduled for the end of August. Specifics willfollow after the spring term. 

Migration Plan

As the current KlickerUZH (v2.0) will become End-of-Life at the end of September 2023, we will no longer be providing a hosted instance thereafter. Before and after the cut-off date, the KlickerUZH v3.0 will be available for public use and a migration of all data (including questions, sessions, and results) will be possible. The migration will still be possible after the KlickerUZH v2.0 has been terminated until at least after the start of the spring term 2024.

If you want to keep access to your data on the KlickerUZH, ensure that you migrate to the new version between August 2023 and March 2024. 

Future of the KlickerUZH Project

The development of the KlickerUZH is funded by the Teaching Center at the Department of Banking and Finance of the University of Zurich. Much of our past development has been funded by project funding (e.g., UZH Innovation Fund/Lehrkredit) and part of our current project is funded by a Swissuniversities P-8 project (DISK4U) where we are evaluating use cases for interaction and gamification and will regularly push new findings to our website and community.

As the aforementioned projects will be coming to an end and the scope, user base, and operating cost of the KlickerUZH has grown by a large margin over the past years, we are currently thinking about sustainable funding concepts that could also involve pricing advanced functionalities (like gamification) or working with sponsoring (on the institutional level). Rest assured that it is our primary goal to make KlickerUZH available for free to our user base at the University of Zurich. We are genuinely interested in your feedback regarding these options and will be communicating more as part of future announcements.

The KlickerUZH is developed by the Teaching Center at the Department of Banking and Finance, University of Zurich. Contact us via klicker.support@uzh.ch or in the official KlickerUZH community.
